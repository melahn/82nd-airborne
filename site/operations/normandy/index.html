Operation Neptune

Operation Neptune was part of Operation Overlord, the invasion of Normandy by the Allied forces. Using both parachute and glider troops, the 82nd Airborne began arriving before daybreak on June 6, 1944 with the goal of blocking the German approaches to the Utah beachhead.

Two other Allied Airborne Divisions also began arriving on that day, the U.S. 101st Airborne Division and the British 6th Airborne Division.

The following documents contain a history of the operation created shortly after the end of the operation and include detailed narratives and maps describing activities of the 82nd Airborne Division during this operation.

Note 1: All pages of the history, including blank pages and covers, are included. The original paper copies are over seventy years old and the paper is frail and faded. I have done my best to enhance the images to improve the readability of the text. No content was changed.

Note 2: The documents were originally classified as 'RESTRICTED' but they have since been declassified.

Note 3: The documents have also been uploaded to the Internet Archive

Note 4: Annex 6 (a map of Division movement on D-Day to D+2) is mentioned in Section IV (list of annexes) but was not included in the bound package.
