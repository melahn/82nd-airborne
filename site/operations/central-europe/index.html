Central Europe

During April and May of 1945, the 82nd Airborne Division helped secure the Allied Victory in Europe. The Division crossed the Rhine and Elbe Rivers, gained the surrender of the German 21st Army and liberated the concentration camp at Ludwiglust.

The following documents contain a history of the 82nd Airborne's action during this period. The documents were created shortly after the end of the war in Europe and include detailed narratives and maps describing activities of the 82nd Airborne Division during this period.

Note 1: All pages of the history, including blank pages and covers, are included. The original paper copies are over seventy years old and the paper is frail and faded. I have done my best to enhance the images to improve the readability of the text. No content was changed.

Note 2: The documents have also been uploaded to the Internet Archive

Note 3: The documents were originally classified as 'RESTRICTED' since they were created shortly after the end of the war in Europe but they have since been declassified.
