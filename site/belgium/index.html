Battle of the Bulge

On December 16, 1944, the Germans launched a massive armored attack on the Allies along thinly held positions in Belgium. Their goal was to reach Antwerp to interrupt Allied supplies and split the British and American forces. What followed became known as The Battle of the Bulge.

The 82nd Airborne was refittiing in France following Operation Market. The Division was quickly transported to Belgium to stem the attack of the Germans. Bitter fighting followed pitting lightly armed Paratroopers against Gernan Panzer Divisions.

The following documents contain a history of the 82nd Airborne's action during the Battle of the Bulge. The documents were created shortly after the end of the battle and include detailed narratives and maps describing activities of the 82nd Airborne Division during the battle.

Note 1:All pages of the history, including blank pages and covers, are included. The original paper copies are over seventy years old and the paper is frail and faded. I have done my best to enhance the images to improve the readability of the text. No content was changed.

Note 2:The documents have also been uploaded to the Internet Archive

Note 3:The documents were originally classied as 'SECRET' since they were created during wartime but they have since been declassified.
